Bakki Iceland
AI Data Centre

A flagship up to 200MW renewable-powered AI infrastructure development

Project Overview

The Bakki AI Data Centre is GIGA-42's flagship development in Iceland: a scalable, 100% renewable-powered, AI-optimised data-centre campus designed to meet surging demand for high-density compute.

Located in North Iceland—one of the coldest, cleanest and most energy-secure regions in Europe—Bakki combines geothermal baseload, natural cooling, robust grid integration, and world-class connectivity.

GIGA-42's vertically integrated approach delivers ultra-low operating costs, rapid scalability and direct access to hyperscale-grade network routes.

Development Plan

Phased Growth to Up to 200MW

Phase 1: 50MW

Initial deployment with modular construction framework that accelerates delivery while reducing project risk.

Phase 2: 100MW

Continued expansion using proven modular construction methods. Increased operational capacity and revenue generation.

Phase 3: 150MW

Further scalable expansion. Refinement of operations and optimization of efficiency metrics.

Phase 4: Up to 200MW

Full capacity deployment by 2030. Steady-state operations with long-term lease revenue and heat recycling partnerships.

ESG & Socio-Economic Impact

Local Employment

Approximately 230 long-term jobs expected at full build-out, supporting local communities and economic development in North Iceland.

Heat Recycling Ecosystem

25–100MW of recoverable heat creates new local industries: aquaculture, greenhouses, district heating, and biotechnology production.

Carbon Leadership

100% geothermal and hydroelectric power delivers an exceptionally low-carbon operating profile. Zero harmful emissions.

Global Market Opportunity

Global AI-compute demand is outpacing supply, with next-generation AI servers requiring dramatically more power and cooling than traditional workloads. Many European hubs face prohibitive constraints: rising power prices, grid congestion, and land scarcity.

Iceland avoids these bottlenecks entirely. With abundant renewable power, natural cooling and available land, Bakki offers hyperscale clients the rare combination of scale, sustainability, and cost leadership.

Why Bakki - Key Differentiators

  • Firm, Baseload Geothermal Power: Bakki has direct access to large-scale geothermal generation, providing continuous, high-load-factor renewable power well suited to AI and data centre operations.
  • Industrial-Ready Site with Grid and Logistics Advantages: Bakki is an established industrial area with access to high-voltage grid infrastructure, campus-scale land, and nearby port facilities, supporting phased delivery and efficient construction.
  • Municipal Alignment and Local Value Creation: Bakki benefits from strong alignment with the local municipality, which supports responsible, long-term development and recognises the role of strategic infrastructure in supporting regional growth and positive local impact.

Project Timeline

2025–2026: Foundation

Equity raise, debt process, power agreements, permitting and final engineering.

2026–2029: Build Phases

Four modular build phases of 50MW each. Parallel workstreams and early procurement reduce delivery timelines.

2030: Full Deployment

Complete up to 200MW deployment and full operational capacity achieved.

2031 Onwards: Operations

Steady-state operations and long-term lease revenue. Heat recycling partnerships and ecosystem development.
