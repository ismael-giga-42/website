Project Overview

The Iceland Renewable Energy Project represents GIGA-42's flagship initiative in the Nordic region, leveraging Iceland's abundant geothermal and hydroelectric resources to create one of the world's largest renewable energy installations.

This groundbreaking project will establish a comprehensive renewable energy ecosystem capable of generating 21 GW of clean electricity, positioning Iceland as a global leader in sustainable energy production and export.

Key Highlights

  • World's largest integrated renewable energy facility
  • Cutting-edge geothermal and hydroelectric technology
  • Zero-carbon energy production at scale
  • Strategic location for European energy export
  • Partnership with local communities and government

Development Timeline

2024-2025: Planning & Permits

Environmental impact assessments, regulatory approvals, and detailed engineering studies. Community engagement and stakeholder consultations.

2025-2028: Phase 1 Construction

Initial geothermal installations and grid infrastructure development. First 5 GW capacity deployment and testing.

2028-2032: Phase 2 Expansion

Hydroelectric facility construction and submarine cable installation. Expansion to 15 GW total capacity.

2032-2040: Full Operations

Complete 21 GW capacity achievement and full European market integration. Ongoing optimization and maintenance.
